Moon

Earth's only natural satellite and our closest celestial neighbor. The Moon's gravity influences our tides and has been an inspiration for countless human achievements in space exploration.

Black Hole

A region of spacetime where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape. These cosmic giants play a crucial role in shaping the evolution of galaxies.

Galaxies

Vast collections of stars, gas, and dark matter bound by gravity. Our Milky Way is just one of billions of galaxies in the observable universe, each telling its own cosmic story.

The Sun

Our life-giving star at the center of the Solar System, the Sun contains 99.86% of the system's mass. This massive ball of hydrogen and helium generates energy through nuclear fusion, powering all life on Earth.
